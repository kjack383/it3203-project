History of the Internet

The Internet has evolved over several decades into the global network we use today. Beginning as a small government research project, it has grown into a worldwide system connecting billions of people for communication, education, business, entertainment, and information sharing.

ARPANET (1969)

ARPANET was created by the U.S. Department of Defense as one of the first packet-switching computer networks. It connected universities and research institutions and became the foundation of today's Internet.

TCP/IP (1983)

In 1983, TCP/IP became the standard communication protocol for networks. It allowed different computer networks to connect and communicate, creating the foundation for the modern Internet.

World Wide Web (1989)

Tim Berners-Lee introduced the World Wide Web in 1989. The Web made the Internet easier to access through browsers, websites, and hyperlinks, allowing millions of people to access information online.

Why the Internet Changed the World

Today the Internet supports online education, banking, shopping, social media, cloud computing, streaming services, and communication around the globe. Continuous improvements in networking technology have made the Internet faster, more reliable, and accessible to billions of users every day.
